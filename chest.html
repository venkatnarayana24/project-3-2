Chest Dumbell Press

Muscles Targeted:
Chest (Pectoralis Major):
The primary muscle group engaged during the dumbbell bench press.
Shoulders (Anterior Deltoids):
These muscles assist in stabilizing the movement.
Triceps:
They play a secondary role in extending the arms during the press.

Cable flies ( lower chest)

Targeted Muscle Group:
The lower chest cable fly specifically focuses on the lower pectoral muscles (pecs). It helps develop the abdominal head of the pectoralis major.

INCLINE PUSHUPS

Muscles Targeted:
Upper Chest:
they place more emphasis on the upper portion of the chest compared to standard push-ups.
Anterior Deltoids (Front Shoulders):
Like standard push-ups, inclined push-ups also engage the front shoulder muscles (anterior deltoids) as they assist in the pushing motion.
Triceps:
Inclined push-ups target the triceps muscles (located at the back of the upper arms) similarly to regular push-ups, as they are engaged to extend the arms during the movement.
